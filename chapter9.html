Chapter 9

The Burdens Love Was Never Meant to Carry

As Jesus continued teaching me through prayer, reflection, and experience, I began recognizing that many of the heaviest burdens in my life had not arrived through a single event. Their weight accumulated gradually over time, settling upon my shoulders one concern, one responsibility, and one disappointment at a time until carrying them felt so familiar that I rarely stopped to question whether they belonged there at all.

That realization surprised me because I had always imagined burdens as obvious hardships that could be easily identified and described. Looking back now, I understand that some burdens arrive quietly. They blend themselves into the routines of daily life and slowly become part of a person's emotional landscape. Their presence feels normal because they have existed for so long, yet normal and healthy are not always the same thing. Sometimes a burden becomes most visible only after exhaustion reveals what has been there all along.

As I reflected upon previous years, I began examining not only what had happened around me but also what had been happening within me. For a long time, I had viewed many of the things I carried as expressions of love. In many respects, that understanding was correct. Love cares deeply about people. Love hopes for healing. Love desires growth, restoration, and well being for those it holds close. Genuine love does not remain indifferent when someone is struggling, nor does it ignore opportunities to encourage positive change.

The difficulty emerged when my desire to love well slowly evolved into a sense of responsibility for outcomes that I could never fully control. Without realizing it, I had invested tremendous emotional energy into concerns that ultimately belonged to someone else. I worried about conversations before they occurred. I worried about situations before they unfolded. I worried about whether enough patience, enough understanding, or enough support might eventually produce a different result than the ones I had already witnessed so many times before.

Those concerns did not originate from selfishness.

They originated from love.

Yet love, however sincere, was never intended to carry every responsibility connected to another person's life.

That distinction became clearer as Jesus continued teaching me the difference between compassion and responsibility. For years, those two concepts often felt intertwined within my mind. Caring deeply about someone made it easy to feel responsible for outcomes that rested entirely beyond my control. The more I loved, the more I felt compelled to help. The more I wanted healing, the more I felt obligated to contribute toward it. The more I hoped for positive change, the more difficult it became to recognize where my responsibility ended and someone else's began.

Experience slowly revealed a truth that was both freeing and uncomfortable.

Caring about another person's choices does not create ownership of those choices.

Wanting someone to grow does not place their growth within your control.

Desiring healing does not make you responsible for producing healing.

Those truths seem straightforward when viewed from a distance, though living them proved far more difficult than understanding them. Part of me believed that greater patience might eventually produce greater change. Part of me believed that enough perseverance might somehow alter circumstances that had resisted change for years. Part of me believed that love, when expressed faithfully enough, should be capable of carrying almost anything.

The passage of time taught me otherwise.

Love possesses tremendous strength, though even healthy love has boundaries established by God. Love can encourage, support, forgive, and remain compassionate through extraordinarily difficult circumstances. Love can walk beside someone through seasons of struggle and uncertainty. Love can offer grace repeatedly while continuing to hope for better days ahead. Yet love cannot become responsible for another person's decisions, and love cannot carry accountability on behalf of someone else.

As those realities became clearer, I began recognizing how many burdens I had been carrying unnecessarily. Some involved fear about the future. Others involved expectations that no human being could realistically fulfill. Still others involved responsibilities that belonged entirely to another person. The weight of those burdens had affected my peace, my energy, my focus, and my emotional well being far more than I had previously acknowledged.

Recognizing those burdens did not immediately remove them. Releasing them required trust. Releasing them required faith. Releasing them required believing that Jesus was capable of carrying what I never could. That process unfolded gradually as He taught me that surrender is not the absence of love. In many ways, surrender represents one of the purest expressions of love because surrender acknowledges the limits of human strength while placing confidence in the unlimited faithfulness of God.

One burden at a time, Jesus taught me to release concerns that had occupied my thoughts for years. One responsibility at a time, He taught me to distinguish between what belonged to me and what belonged to Him. One fear at a time, He reminded me that the future had never depended upon my ability to carry every possible outcome upon my shoulders.

Looking back now, I understand that the title of this book reflects far more than a collection of memories or a particular season of life. The title reflects a realization that emerged through years of prayer, reflection, disappointment, growth, and grace. Love had been carrying fears that belonged to Jesus. Love had been carrying responsibilities that belonged to others. Love had been carrying worries about a future that remained safely in God's hands.

That realization brought freedom, not because every question had been answered or because every wound had completely healed, but because I finally understood something Jesus had been patiently teaching me all along. He had never asked me to carry the world upon my shoulders. He had never asked me to become responsible for outcomes beyond my control. He had never asked me to bear burdens that belonged in His hands.

He had simply asked me to follow Him.

In following Him, I discovered that the strongest love is not the love that carries everything. The strongest love is the love that trusts Jesus enough to place every burden, every fear, and every unanswered question into His hands, believing that He can carry what we never could.
