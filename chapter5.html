Chapter 5

The Cost of Carrying It

By that point, a different question had begun finding its way into my thoughts. The question no longer centered upon understanding another person's behavior. Years had already been spent searching for explanations, examining circumstances, extending grace, and hoping that difficult patterns would eventually lose their influence. The more I reflected upon those efforts, the more I realized that another question deserved my attention.

What has all of this been costing me?

The answer did not arrive immediately.

Some questions require distance before they can be answered honestly. Living inside an experience often makes its effects difficult to measure because gradual burdens rarely announce themselves as they accumulate. They settle quietly upon the heart, becoming so familiar that their weight begins to feel normal.

Looking back now, I can see that certain changes had been occurring within me long before I recognized them.

Weariness had become familiar.

Hypervigilance had become familiar.

The habit of constantly evaluating situations before they unfolded had become familiar.

The tendency to anticipate emotional reactions before they occurred had become familiar.

Many of those behaviors developed so gradually that I hardly noticed them at first. They appeared practical in the moment. They seemed helpful. They often felt necessary. Only later did I realize how much energy was being invested into managing situations that had never been mine to manage.

One of the greatest costs involved peace.

Peace had not disappeared entirely. Moments of joy still existed. Laughter still existed. Meaningful conversations still existed. Yet peace increasingly felt conditional rather than stable. Calm often seemed connected to circumstances remaining favorable rather than resting upon a deeper sense of security.

That realization troubled me.

Jesus never intended peace to function that way.

The peace He offers remains available in every season, including seasons marked by uncertainty. The peace He provides does not depend upon perfect circumstances or predictable outcomes. Looking back, I recognize how often my attention had become fixed upon external situations while Jesus patiently invited me to anchor myself more deeply in Him.

Another cost involved emotional energy.

Every difficult cycle required attention.

Every conflict required processing.

Every apology required evaluation.

Every promise required hope.

Every disappointment required recovery.

None of those experiences existed in isolation. Together, they created an invisible burden that followed me into places where it did not belong. The burden followed me into quiet moments. The burden followed me into prayer. The burden followed me into ordinary days that should have belonged entirely to other responsibilities.

Perhaps the most painful realization involved my child.

As a parent, protecting a child becomes instinctive. Seeing conflict unfold in front of my child created concerns that extended far beyond my own feelings. Certain moments affected me differently because they no longer involved only my experience. They involved someone I loved deeply and wanted to protect from unnecessary hurt.

That realization forced me to confront questions I could no longer postpone.

What examples were being observed?

What lessons were being learned?

What impact might these experiences carry into the future?

Those questions mattered because love protects. Love pays attention. Love takes responsibility for the well being of those entrusted to its care.

The more honestly I examined those realities, the more I recognized that the cost extended beyond frustration, disappointment, or emotional exhaustion. The cost affected my peace, my energy, my focus, my confidence, and my sense of stability in ways I had not fully acknowledged before.

Yet amid those realizations, another truth continued emerging.

Jesus had never abandoned me.

Throughout every difficult season, every painful realization, and every unanswered question, His presence remained constant. While I struggled to understand the cost of what I was carrying, He already understood it completely.

He saw the burden.

He saw the weariness.

He saw the concern.

He saw the tears.

Most importantly, He saw the person carrying all of it.

Recognizing the cost was painful.

Recognizing it was also necessary.

Healing rarely begins until honesty arrives.
