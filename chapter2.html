Chapter 2

What Love Was Carrying

The drive ended.

Conversation eventually faded into silence. Destinations were reached. Goodbyes were exchanged. Life continued moving forward according to the schedule that had already been established long before that evening began. From the outside, nothing appeared remarkable. Another difficult interaction had come and gone. Another uncomfortable conversation had found its place among countless other conversations that had occupied previous years.

Yet something felt different.

The difference had nothing to do with the gas station.

The difference had nothing to do with traffic.

The difference had nothing to do with a sudden stop, a detour, or the frustration that followed.

For the first time in a very long time, I found myself looking beyond the details of the moment and focusing on something larger. My attention shifted away from the event itself and toward the pattern that surrounded it.

Sleep provided distance, though distance did not provide answers.

The following morning brought clarity that had remained hidden beneath the emotions of the previous day. Looking back across the entire sequence of events, I struggled to identify a moment that justified the level of anger, accusation, and frustration that eventually emerged. Every attempt to understand the situation led me to the same conclusion. The gas station was never the issue.

The drive was never the issue.

Something much deeper had been unfolding for years.

That realization carried its own weight because it challenged a habit I had developed without realizing it. Whenever conflict appeared, my first instinct was almost always to examine myself. Questions immediately followed.

What could I have done differently?

What should I have said?

What should I have avoided saying?

How could I have handled the situation better?

Those questions were familiar companions. They had accompanied me through countless disappointments, misunderstandings, and difficult conversations. Looking inward had become second nature. Honest self examination possesses value, yet honest self examination can become unhealthy when every situation begins and ends with the assumption that the answer must always be found within yourself.

This time felt different.

The more I reflected upon the previous day, the more I realized that I was not wrestling with a single event. I was wrestling with years of accumulated experiences that shared a common thread. Different circumstances had produced remarkably similar outcomes. Different settings had produced remarkably similar emotions. Different conversations had somehow arrived at remarkably familiar destinations.

The pattern was no longer hiding.

The pattern was no longer isolated.

The pattern had become impossible to ignore.

That realization forced me to ask a difficult question.

What had love been carrying all these years?

The answer did not arrive immediately. Reflection rarely works that way. Truth often reveals itself slowly, allowing one realization to illuminate another until a clearer picture begins to emerge. Looking back across previous years, I began recognizing burdens that had quietly accumulated without ever being acknowledged.

Love had been carrying hope.

Hope that difficult moments would become less frequent.

Hope that apologies would lead to lasting change.

Hope that tomorrow would look different from yesterday.

Hope that patterns would eventually break.

Love had also been carrying disappointment.

Each promise created expectation. Each apology restored optimism. Each calm season encouraged the belief that meaningful change might finally be taking root. Yet when familiar cycles returned, disappointment followed close behind, leaving questions that never seemed to find lasting answers.

Love had been carrying confusion.

Good days existed. Meaningful conversations existed. Genuine affection existed. Laughter existed. Those moments mattered because they were real. Their presence complicated everything. Beauty and brokenness occupied the same story. Hope and hurt often shared the same space. The coexistence of both realities made understanding the larger picture far more difficult than many people realize.

Love had been carrying responsibility that never belonged to it.

Somewhere along the journey, compassion became intertwined with responsibility. Patience became intertwined with endurance. Grace became intertwined with carrying burdens that extended beyond my ability to resolve. Without realizing it, I had begun assuming responsibility for outcomes that ultimately belonged to someone else.

That burden grew heavier with time.

No amount of love can make another person choose accountability.

No amount of patience can create change within someone who refuses to confront their own patterns.

No amount of hope can carry a relationship by itself.

Those realizations were painful because they challenged beliefs I had carried for years. They forced me to separate compassion from responsibility and grace from self abandonment. Such lessons are rarely learned quickly. Most arrive through experience, prayer, reflection, and time.

Jesus remained present throughout every part of that process.

He remained present when clarity felt distant.

He remained present when questions seemed greater than answers.

He remained present when my own thoughts became difficult to untangle.

Long before I recognized what love was carrying, Jesus already knew.

He saw every burden.

He saw every prayer.

He saw every disappointment.

He saw every attempt to hold together what felt increasingly fragile.

Most importantly, He saw the person carrying the weight.

Looking back now, I understand that the greatest burden was not any single event. The greatest burden was the constant effort required to reconcile what I hoped was true with what continued proving true.

That struggle consumed more energy than I realized.

That struggle occupied more space within my heart than I understood.

That struggle became one of the heaviest things love carried.

Yet through every season, whether clarity felt near or impossibly distant, one truth remained unchanged.

Jesus saw everything.

Long before the pattern became visible to me, Jesus had already seen the entire picture.
